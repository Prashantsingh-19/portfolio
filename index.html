about me

Prashant Singh

MBA Candidate · Product & Strategy · Building at the intersection of data and decisions

You're probably wondering why a physics grad is building dashboards and data products, so let me walk you through my calculated but fun journey.

I fell in love with physics while preparing for engineering entrance — so much so that, even after managing to crack a few colleges, I decided to drop engineering as a plan entirely and went to pursue Physics (Hons.) instead. But then you might wonder, why MBA? Why not MSc, why not PhD?

I fell in love with physics because of how it lets you imagine. How a moving electron generates a magnetic field. How a moving electron shows its dual nature. The list is wide, but the crux is this: it lets you imagine. And as someone once rightly said, imagination has no bounds. When you couple physics' ability to make you imagine with your own imagination, you can make something that is yours to begin with — but in order to sustain in the real world, physics has rules, it has constraints, but it's yours.

So I thought, since I have the foundation of first principles, why not use it to imagine something? Not a machine though — I left engineering long back. Can my thinking draw out something impactful in business as well?

The best way to find out was to start doing it.
